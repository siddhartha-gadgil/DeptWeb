Balanced words on a finite alphabet are those words in which every
letter of the alphabet occurs the same number of times. The notion of
descents and major index extends in a natural way to words. It is known
that the bivariate generating polynomials for descents and major index
over balanced words on the alphabet $[k]$ with $n$ occurrences each is
palindromic, but a bijective proof has been missing even for balanced
binary words. In this talk, I will first present a bijection for
balanced binary words and then recursively extend the bijection for all
balanced words. I will also describe some of the properties of this
bijection on restrictions of balanced words like permutations.

This is a [joint work](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12919) with Arvind Ayyer (IISc).
