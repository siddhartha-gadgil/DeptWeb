The Hopf-Rinow theorem is a fundamental result in Riemannian geometry relating metric completeness, properness, and geodesic 
completeness. We recall the many reasons why it does not hold in Lorentzian geometry. Then we present our joint work with

viewed as an extension of the metric part of the Hopf-Rinow theorem to Lorentzian geometry. We end with some thoughts about the
