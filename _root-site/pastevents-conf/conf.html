International Conference on Dynamical Systems

Department of Mathematics, Indian Institute of Science

The International Conference on Dynamical Systems will be held at the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, India during the period January 10 - 15, 1997. The Conference will focus on new developments in the field of dynamical systems and its applications. The following four areas have been selected for special emphasis:

  • Control of Chaos
  • Mathematical theory of dynamical systems
  • Nonlinear dynamics in plasma physics
  • Nonlinear dynamics in accelerator physics

Mini lecture series will be organized in these four areas in addition to the sessions for invited papers.

Those intersted in participating in the Conference should contact Dr. G. Rangarajan (preferably by e-mail) before November 15, 1996. Requests for participation by students should be accompanied by a recommendation letter from a faculty member.

The Conference organizers will provide local hospitality (boarding and lodging) during the Conference. However, there is no support available towards international travel. Some travel support for Indian participants may be available.

Bangalore (the capital of the state of Karnataka) has a pleasant climate during the month of January. It is well connected by air to other Indian cities like Bombay, Delhi and Madras. There are several ancient and historical monuments within a few hours drive from Bangalore. A sight seeing trip to one of these sites is being planned during the Conference. There are also wildlife sanctuaries nearby. January would also be an ideal time to visit other places in India like Delhi, Agra (Taj Mahal), Jaipur, Bombay etc.
