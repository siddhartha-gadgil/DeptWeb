Introduction to Modular Forms

The modular group and its subgroups, the fundamental domain. Modular forms, examples, Eisenstein series, cusp forms. Valence (dimension) formula, Petersson inner product. Hecke operators. L-functios: definition, analytic continution and functional equation.

 

Recommended books

  • Serre, J.P., A Course in Arithmetic, Graduate Texts in Mathematics no. 7 Springer-Verlag, 1996.
  • Koblitz, N., Introdution to Modular Forms, Graduate Texts in Mathematics no. 97, Springer-Verlag, 1984.
  • Iwaniec, H., Topics in Classical Automorphic Forms, Graduate Texts in Mathematics 17, AMS, 1997.
  • Diamond, F. and Schurman, J., A First Course in Modular Forms, Graduate Texts in Mathematics no. 228, Springer-Verlag, 2005.

 

PREREQUISITES:

MA 224 (Complex Analysis) or equivalent
