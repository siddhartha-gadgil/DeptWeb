Biography

About

A. K. Nandakumaran is a Professor in the Department of Mathematics at the Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru, where he formerly served as Chair. He obtained his Master's degree from Calicut University, Kerala, and completed his doctoral work at the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research and the Indian Institute of Science.

His research is in analysis and partial differential equations, with work on homogenization in oscillating domains, exact controllability and optimal control, differential games, and inverse problems in tomography. He also works on applications that arise in biomedical optics and structural health assessment.

He has written three textbooks in the Cambridge–IISc Series published by Cambridge University Press, delivered five NPTEL lecture series, and is a Fellow of the National Academy of Sciences India (NASI). He served as Convener of the Kishore Vaigyanik Prothsahan Yojana (KVPY) from 2019 to 2022 and as Regional Coordinator of the Karnataka Regional Mathematical Olympiad (KRMO) from 1998 to 2008.

Areas of Work

Research Interests

Homogenization

Asymptotic analysis of PDEs posed on periodically oscillating and perforated domains; unfolding operators; H-convergence.

Control & Controllability

Exact and approximate controllability for wave and heat equations; optimal control problems in oscillating geometries.

Partial Differential Equations

PDE theory, including elliptic, parabolic, and hyperbolic equations, together with functional analytic methods.

Differential Games

Zero-sum stochastic differential games; viscosity solutions of Hamilton–Jacobi–Isaacs equations; relaxed strategies.

Inverse Problems

Recovery of mechanical and optical parameters from boundary measurements; diffuse correlation tomography.

Tomography & Imaging

Ultrasound-modulated optical tomography, speckle contrast methods, and biomedical optics in turbid media.

A graduate-level text on ordinary differential equations. It covers existence and uniqueness, linear systems, boundary value problems, stability theory, and worked examples.

Partial Differential Equations: Classical Theory with a Modern Touch

A. K. Nandakumaran & P. S. Datti

Cambridge–IISc Series, Cambridge University Press

A text on classical PDE theory and its functional analytic foundations, including distributions, Sobolev spaces, and weak formulations. It treats elliptic, parabolic, and hyperbolic equations with applications to physical systems.
